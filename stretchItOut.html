Stretch it out!

Stretching has been shown to increase serotonin levels — i.e., the hormone that helps stabilize our mood, reduce stress, and overall makes us feel good — which causes a decrease in depression and anxiety.

Simple Stretches to Try:

  • Neck Rolls: Sit or stand tall, gently drop your chin to your chest, then slowly roll your head in a circle to loosen neck muscles.
  • Shoulder Shrugs: Lift both shoulders up toward your ears, hold briefly, then release and lower. Repeat several times to relieve tension.
  • Arm Circles: Extend arms out to sides, make small circles forward and backward to warm up shoulders.
  • Standing Side Stretch: Stand with feet hip-width apart, reach one arm overhead and lean to the opposite side, feeling a stretch along your side. Switch sides.
  • Seated Forward Bend: Sit with legs extended, reach forward toward your toes, keeping your back straight to stretch your hamstrings and back.
  • Cat-Cow Stretch: On hands and knees, alternate arching your back (cat) and dipping it down (cow) to mobilize your spine.
  • Child’s Pose: Kneel on the floor, sit back on your heels, stretch arms forward and lower your forehead to the ground for a gentle back stretch.
  • Wrist Stretch: Extend one arm in front, palm up. Use the other hand to gently pull fingers down and back. Switch sides.
  • Seated Spinal Twist: Sit with legs extended, bend one knee over the opposite leg, twist torso toward bent knee. Hold and switch sides.
  • Chest Opener: Clasp hands behind your back, straighten arms, and lift hands while opening chest and squeezing shoulder blades together.
  • Butterfly Stretch: Sit, bring soles of feet together, let knees fall outward. Hold feet and gently press knees toward the floor.
  • Figure Four Stretch: Lie on your back, cross one ankle over opposite knee, pull the uncrossed leg toward your chest. Switch sides.
  • Hip Flexor Stretch: Kneel on one knee, other foot in front. Lean forward to stretch front of hip. Switch sides.
  • Downward Dog: Start on hands and knees, lift hips up and back, straighten legs and arms, forming an inverted V shape.
  • Upward Dog: Lie face down, place hands under shoulders, press up lifting chest and thighs off the ground, arching back gently.
  • Triceps Stretch: Raise one arm overhead, bend elbow, use other hand to gently pull elbow toward head. Switch sides.
  • Side Lunge Stretch: Stand with feet wide, bend one knee and lean to that side, keeping other leg straight. Switch sides.
  • Standing Toe Touch: Stand tall, slowly bend forward at hips, reaching for your toes. Hold and rise slowly.
  • Glute Stretch: Lie on your back, cross one ankle over opposite knee, pull uncrossed leg toward chest. Switch sides.
  • Shoulder Stretch Across Body: Bring one arm across chest, use other arm to pull it closer. Hold and switch sides.
  • Standing IT Band Stretch: Stand, cross one leg behind the other, reach arm overhead and lean to opposite side. Switch sides.
  • Seated Shoulder Squeeze: Sit, clasp hands behind back, squeeze shoulder blades together and lift arms slightly.
  • Supine Twist: Lie on back, bring knees to chest, drop knees to one side, extend arms out. Hold and switch sides.
  • Standing Wall Stretch: Stand facing a wall, place hands on wall, step back and lower chest toward floor for a shoulder stretch.
  • Forearm Stretch: Extend one arm, palm down, use other hand to gently pull fingers back toward body. Switch sides.
  • Standing Hip Circles: Stand with feet hip-width apart, hands on hips, gently rotate hips in circles both directions.
  • Seated Figure Four: Sit, cross one ankle over opposite knee, lean forward gently to stretch hips. Switch sides.

Try holding each stretch for 15-30 seconds and remember to breathe deeply. Listen to your body and stretch gently!
